Using ML to predict CFD characteristics

What is the purpose?

The use of machine learning (ML) in computational fluid dynamics (CFD) is gaining traction due to its ability to enhance accuracy, reduce computational costs, and improve understanding of fluid flow phenomena. ML algorithms can effectively learn from data to make predictions about fluid behavior, leading to more accurate simulations, particularly for complex flows that pose challenges for traditional modeling methods. Additionally, ML can be employed to develop surrogate models that approximate CFD simulation results with reduced computational demands, thereby streamlining the simulation process. Furthermore, ML can be applied to analyze CFD simulation results to gain deeper insights into fluid flow patterns, fostering the development of more efficient and effective engineering solutions.

ANN? ANFIS?

Artificial Neural Network (ANN) is more widely used and easier to implement than Adaptive Neuro-Fuzzy Inference System (ANFIS). ANNs are more flexible and can learn complex patterns from data, while ANFIS can handle imprecise and uncertain data. However, ANFIS can be more computationally expensive and difficult to interpret.

Study on Ventilated Cavity incorporating ML(in preparation)

Study on ventilated cavity has the potential to significantly improve the design and optimization of thermal systems by providing a comprehensive understanding of the factors influencing heat transfer and pressure drop characteristics. This findings can be applied to various applications, including heat exchangers, air conditioning systems, and electronic cooling systems.

Study on Porous-Media (in preparation)

CFD simulations of porous media are often computationally expensive, especially for complex geometries. ML can be used to reduce the computational cost of these simulations by providing reduced-order models or by accelerating the solution process.
