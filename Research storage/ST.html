Analysis of an AACAES using PCM as TES with FDM method

What is the purpose?

Energy storage is the process of storing energy for later use. It is a critical component of the modern energy system, as it allows us to store excess energy when it is generated and use it when it is needed. There are many different types of energy storage, including pumped hydro storage, batteries, flywheels, and compressed air energy storage (CAES). CAES is a type of energy storage that stores energy in the form of compressed air. It is a very promising technology, as it is scalable, efficient, and has a long lifespan.

What is AACAES

Advanced adiabatic compressed air energy storage (AA-CAES) is a technology for storing energy in the form of compressed air. It is an advanced form of compressed air energy storage (CAES) that uses a thermal storage system to store the heat generated during the compression process. This allows AA-CAES to achieve higher round-trip efficiencies than traditional CAES.

PCM as TES

Phase change materials (PCMs) are a promising technology for thermal energy storage (TES). PCMs store energy in the form of latent heat, which is the energy absorbed or released when a material changes phase, such as from solid to liquid or liquid to gas. This makes PCMs very efficient at storing thermal energy, as they can store a large amount of energy in a relatively small volume.

Study on AACAES (submitted in ENCON)

AACAES systems typically use a multi-stage compression process to achieve higher compression ratios and therefore higher energy densities. The use of multiple compression stages allows for more efficient intercooling, which reduces the heat generated during compression and improves the overall efficiency of the system.
