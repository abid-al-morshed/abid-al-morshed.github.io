Using thermally sensitive controller to control inflow

Why?

Controlling inflow is crucial in various applications, from maintaining a desired temperature in water heating systems to regulating pressure in pneumatic systems. Proportional, integral, and derivative (PID) controllers have emerged as the most effective approach to achieving precise and stable inflow control. PID controllers combine the three control actions, proportional, integral, and derivative, to address the challenges of inflow regulation. Proportional controllers adjust the inflow based on the immediate error between the setpoint and actual inflow, while integral controllers eliminate steady-state error by accounting for accumulated error over time. Derivative controllers, on the other hand, minimize overshoot and oscillations by considering the rate of change of the error. PID controllers' versatility and effectiveness have led to their widespread adoption in diverse applications, including water heating, pneumatic systems, and combustion processes..

Why dual flow modulator?

The configuration of double rotating cylinders in a vented cavity has potential applications in various fields, including heat transfer enhancement, fluid mixing, and energy conversion. The introduction of rotating cylinders introduces vortices and chaotic flow patterns within the cavity, enhancing fluid mixing and overall heat transfer efficiency. Additionally, the vented cavity allows for efficient heat dissipation and reduces the potential for stagnation zones.

Tuning a controller

Tuning a controller is like adjusting the speed and sensitivity of a thermostat to maintain a comfortable room temperature. Proper tuning prevents the temperature from fluctuating too much, which can damage the thermostat or cause discomfort. By tuning the controller correctly, you can ensure the room stays at the desired temperature without wasting energy or causing damage.

Study on using P,PI, PID to control inflow (in preparation)

Our study investigates the effectiveness of three different temperature-dependent controllers (P, PI, and PID) in regulating the flow rate of Cu-water nanofluid through a vented cavity with double rotating hot cylinders. The study utilizes a finite element method to solve the governing equations and examines the impact of different controller gain factors on the flow and thermal properties.
